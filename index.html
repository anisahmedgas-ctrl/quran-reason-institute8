The Problem

We were taught about Islam.
Nobody taught us to understand it.

For centuries the default model has been: memorise, repeat, obey. The Qur'an itself calls this out. It commands critical thought, evidence, and reflection — not the unquestioned following of scholars and inherited tradition.

Core Reasoning

Questions the Qur'an
forces us to ask

These are not fringe positions. They come directly from the Qur'anic text, supported by specific verses. Read them, reason through them, decide for yourself.

1
Masjid — Rethought

A Masjid is not a building. It's a system.

The Qur'an describes masjids being used for harm (9:107), people being blocked from them (2:114), and them belonging only to God (72:18). None of this makes sense if a masjid is simply a building. The Qur'an defines it by function — not walls.

"The masjids belong to God, so do not invoke anyone alongside God." — 72:18
2
Salat — Rethought

Salat is not ritual prayer. It's a system of reform.

The root of 'salat' means connection, support, and moral action. The Qur'an never describes the specific postures, timings, or Arabic phrases of traditional prayer. These came from hadith, recorded 200+ years later — not from the Qur'an itself.

"Establish salat — salat prevents indecency and wrongdoing." — 29:45
3
Hadith — The Evidence

The Qur'an itself rejects hadith as a religious source.

The Qur'an calls itself "complete, detailed, and sufficient" (6:115). It warns against following "other hadith" (7:185, 45:6, 77:50). Even the first four Caliphs refused to allow hadith to be written down. This is not a fringe view — it is documented history.

"In what hadith after this will they believe?" — 77:50
4
Reason — The Command

The Qur'an commands critical thinking. Constantly.

The Qur'an uses 'aql (intellect), tafakkur (reflection), tadabbur (deep thought), and nazar (observation) hundreds of times. It condemns those who follow tradition blindly (2:170). It praises those who observe, question, and reason (3:190). This is the core of what Islam actually asks.

"Indeed, the worst of creatures are those who do not reason." — 8:22
5
Siyam — Rethought

Ramadan "fasting" is about Qur'an study, not food.

The Qur'an introduces Ramadan as "the month in which the Qur'an was revealed" (2:185). The command is to engage with it. The word 'siyam' means purposeful self-restraint and focus. The elaborate food-fasting institution is a later cultural addition.

"The month of Ramadan in which the Qur'an was revealed as guidance." — 2:185
6
Shahada — Rethought

The Qur'anic testimony has no name but God's.

The Qur'an presents only one testimony: "La ilaha illa Allah" — there is no god but God. Adding "Muhammadur rasulullah" as a paired statement has no Qur'anic basis. This is a 22-point case backed entirely by Qur'anic verses.

"God bears witness that there is no deity except Him." — 3:18
Our Approach

Reason is not
the enemy of faith.

The Qur'an uses the word 'aql (intellect/reason) 49 times. It uses tafakkur (reflection) 18 times. It asks "do you not see?" and "do you not think?" dozens of times. This is not a book that asks for blind belief.

"Those who reflect on the creation of the heavens and earth say: Our Lord, You did not create this without purpose." — 3:191

Every claim we make is backed by a specific verse. Every argument can be tested. We do not ask you to accept anything on authority.
