Why This Institute Exists

1,400 years
of drift.

When Moses left his people for 40 days, they built an idol and started worshipping it. God recorded that in Qur'an 2:51 — not as ancient history, but as a warning for every community that follows.

Now think about 1,400 years. Political dynasties needing obedient populations. Clergy paid to maintain the status quo. Hadith compiled 200 years after the Prophet died by people who never met him. Zoroastrian rituals rebranded as Islam. Scholars elevated to lords.

The Qur'an calls itself complete, detailed, and sufficient. It was the first thing they abandoned.

We started this institute because we read the Qur'an and couldn't find most of what we were told was mandatory. So we went back to the source — the Arabic text, the root words, the historical record. What we found changed everything.

How the Original Message Was
Systematically Replaced

The drift from the Qur'an wasn't accidental. We found it happened through specific, documented processes — verifiable in history and confirmed by the Qur'anic text itself.

01
Conspiracy One
God replaced with human intermediaries

The direct relationship between a person and God was broken by inserting human middlemen. Messengers became objects of worship. Pirs, saints, Imams, and scholars became the new authorities. The Qur'an is clear: sovereignty belongs to God alone and no human stands between you and your Creator.

Qur'an 9:31
02
Conspiracy Two
Islam split into sects from within

A two-pronged operation: political weakening through manufactured sectarian division, and ideological corruption through fabricated hadith and infiltration from Persian, Jewish, and Zoroastrian traditions. The result: hundreds of sects each claiming exclusive truth, none returning to the Book that started it all.

Qur'an 6:159
03
Conspiracy Three
The Qur'anic text itself was attacked

Groups promoted the idea that the present Qur'an is corrupted, that its words carry hidden meanings only Imams can access, and that the real text was suppressed. The Qur'an demolishes every version of this claim. God personally guaranteed its preservation.

Qur'an 15:9
04
Conspiracy Four
Sufism replaced action with passivity

Sufism entered Muslim civilization wearing Islamic terminology, but its roots are in Greek philosophy, Neo-Platonism, and Hindu mysticism. It replaced the Qur'an's call to establish justice with passive inner experience and devotion to saints — one of the most effective tools ever used to neutralize Islamic social reform.

Qur'an 2:44
05
Conspiracy Five
Namaaz replaced Salat

The word "Salat" (root: ص-ل-و) means to follow closely, pursue, remain attached — a complete system of conduct. The five-times ritual called Namaaz has roots in pre-Islamic Zoroastrian practice, institutionalized by the Umayyad dynasty as a tool of control. The Prophet established Salat. What most Muslims practice today is Namaaz.

Qur'an 29:45
06
Conspiracy Six
Human sovereignty replaced God's

The Umayyad dynasty imported the Christian concept of "divine right of kings" into Islam. Democracy, autocracy, theocracy, and clergy-run systems all share the same flaw: they replace God's sovereignty with human sovereignty. The Qur'an says the command belongs to God alone, exercised through His Book.

Qur'an 12:40

Our Core Research Findings

What We Found When We
Actually Read the Qur'an

Not fringe positions. These come directly from the Qur'anic text. We checked every verse, traced every root word, and followed the evidence wherever it led.

1
Salat — What It Really Means
Salat is not ritual prayer. It is a complete system of conduct.

We studied all 102 occurrences of "salat" in the Qur'an. Not one describes specific physical postures, specific daily timings, or specific Arabic phrases to recite. The Arabic root (ص-ل-و) means to follow closely, to pursue, to remain attached — confirmed in Lane's Lexicon, the definitive classical Arabic dictionary. Salat describes how you conduct your entire life according to divine guidance. Not a five-minute physical exercise performed five times a day.

"Establish salat — salat prevents indecency and wrongdoing." — 29:45
2
Hadith — The Hard Evidence
The Qur'an names hadith as the problem — using that exact word.

The Qur'an calls itself complete (6:115), detailed (6:114), sufficient (29:51), and preserved (15:9). It warns against following "other hadith" — using that exact Arabic word — in 7:185, 45:6, and 77:50. The first four Caliphs all refused to allow hadith to be written. Bukhari was born 194 years after the Prophet. This is documented, undisputed history.

"In what hadith after this will they believe?" — 77:50
3
Reasoning — The Obligation
The Qur'an makes critical thinking a religious obligation. Repeatedly.

The word "aql" — intellect and reason — appears 49 times. "Tafakkur" — deep reflection — appears 18 times. The Qur'an asks "do you not see?" and "do you not think?" dozens of times. It condemns following forefathers blindly in 2:170 and 5:104. The very book most Muslims claim to follow commands the one thing most Muslim institutions punish: independent critical thought.

"Indeed, the worst of creatures are those who do not reason." — 8:22
4
Ramadan — The Actual Command
Ramadan was about engaging the Qur'an — not restricting food.

The Qur'an introduces Ramadan with: "The month in which the Qur'an was revealed as guidance for mankind" (2:185). "Siyam" means purposeful restraint and focused attention. In 19:26, Mary observed siyam as a vow of silence — while she ate and drank. The food-fasting institution replaced the actual command: deep, sustained engagement with the Book revealed in that month.

"The month of Ramadan in which the Qur'an was revealed as guidance." — 2:185
5
Shahada — What's Actually in the Text
The Qur'anic testimony contains only one name: God's.

The Qur'an presents exactly one testimony: "La ilaha illa Allah" — there is no god but God. That's 3:18, and it's God Himself taking that testimony along with the angels and those with knowledge. Adding "Muhammadur rasulullah" as a required paired statement has no Qur'anic basis. Archaeological evidence shows the dual shahada evolved as a political tool of the Umayyad dynasty.

"God bears witness that there is no deity except Him." — 3:18
"When it is said to them, follow what God has revealed — they say: We will follow what we found our fathers doing."
— Qur'an 2:170
Hadith Refutations

15 Arguments for Hadith.
15 Qur'anic Answers.

Every standard argument made for hadith authority — answered directly from the Qur'an. Not our interpretation. The actual text. Bukhari was born 194 years after the Prophet. Muslim was born 206 years after.

01
The Argument
"Obeying the Messenger means following Hadith"

The Qur'an defines obeying the Messenger as obeying the revelation he delivered. His duty was to convey — not create a second religious source. No verse equates obeying the Messenger with obeying books compiled two centuries after his death.

Qur'an 24:54 · 6:50 · 5:99
02
The Argument
"The Messenger was sent to explain the Qur'an — so we need Hadith"

The word in 16:44 is "li-tubayyina" — to make evident, not to add external content. The Qur'an states its verses are perfected (11:1) and clarifies itself (25:33). The Prophet's role was to deliver and live by the Qur'an.

Qur'an 16:44 · 25:33 · 11:1
03
The Argument
"Hadith provides the details of Salat and Zakat the Qur'an doesn't specify"

"We did not leave anything out of the Book" (6:38). When you understand Salat through its actual Arabic root — the Qur'an is entirely sufficient. "This Book is a clarification for all things" (16:89).

Qur'an 6:38 · 29:45 · 16:89
04
The Argument
"The Qur'an is too general — Hadith fills in the necessary specifics"

The Qur'an calls itself "mufassal" — fully detailed (41:3) and "tibyan" — clarification for all things (16:89). God asks directly: "Is it not sufficient for them that We sent down the Book?" (29:51).

Qur'an 29:51 · 41:3 · 6:114
05
The Argument
"God preserved Hadith as part of preserving the religion"

God promises to preserve exactly one thing: the Qur'an (15:9). No other source has this divine protection. Hadith is a human compilation — God never guaranteed its accuracy or preservation.

Qur'an 15:9
06
The Argument
"The Prophet had independent legal authority beyond the Qur'an"

The Prophet himself said: "I do not follow except what is revealed to me" (6:50). He judged only by God's revelation (5:48). His authority came from the Qur'an — nothing else.

Qur'an 6:50 · 5:48 · 5:49
07
The Argument
"The companions followed Hadith — so should we"

The Qur'an explicitly rejects this: "They followed their forefathers even if they understood nothing" (2:170). Truth is not determined by historical precedent — it is determined by what God revealed.

Qur'an 2:170 · 6:116
08
The Argument
"Rejecting Hadith means rejecting the Prophet himself"

"The Messenger's duty is only to convey clearly" (5:99). Verse 59:7 — misused constantly — specifically addresses distribution of war spoils. It is not a general mandate for hadith authority.

Qur'an 5:99 · 59:7
09
The Argument
"Isnad science verifies which Hadith are authentic"

Chains of oral narrators spanning 200 years remain human conjecture — not divine preservation. "Do not follow what you have no knowledge of" (17:36). No chain of human memory equals a divine guarantee.

Qur'an 17:36 · 53:28
10
The Argument
"Hadith fills the gaps the Qur'an left open"

"We did not leave anything out of the Book" (6:38). "Today I have completed your system for you" (5:3). You cannot simultaneously believe 5:3 and claim the Qur'an is insufficient.

Qur'an 6:38 · 5:3
11
The Argument
"Sunnah is a second form of divine revelation"

53:3-4 refers to the Qur'an being recited — not personal sayings. If Sunnah were revelation, God would have preserved it the same way He preserved the Qur'an. He did not. That absence is itself the evidence.

Qur'an 53:3–4 · 6:50 · 15:9
12
The Argument
"The Prophet's legal judgments prove law exists outside the Qur'an"

"So judge between them by what God has sent down" (5:48). The Qur'an commands the Prophet to judge only by revelation and warns him against using tradition or personal preference.

Qur'an 5:48 · 5:49
13
The Argument
"Scholarly consensus (Ijma) confirms Hadith authority"

"If you follow most of those on Earth, they will mislead you from God's path" (6:116). "They have taken their scholars and priests as lords besides God" (9:31). The Qur'an directly warns against exactly this kind of reasoning.

Qur'an 6:116 · 9:31
14
The Argument
"The same people who preserved the Qur'an preserved the Hadith"

God preserves the Qur'an — not human transmitters. The Qur'an never equates human sincerity with divine preservation. These are categorically different claims.

Qur'an 15:9
15
The Argument
"You simply cannot practice Islam without Hadith"

"This Qur'an guides to that which is most upright" (17:9). "We have sent the Book as clarification for all things" (16:89). The Qur'an declares itself practical, complete, and sufficient for every generation.

Qur'an 17:9 · 16:89 · 43:43
"Most of them follow nothing but conjecture. And conjecture can never replace the truth."
— Qur'an 10:36
